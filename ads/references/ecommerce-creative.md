# E-Commerce Creative Playbook

> Updated: 2026-04-01
> Source: Used by creative-strategist, ads-generate, and ads-create skills

## Overview

5 campaign types with banana domain modes, aspect ratios, copy frameworks, and budget allocation guidance for e-commerce advertisers.

---

## Campaign Types

### 1. Product Launch

**Goal:** Drive awareness and first-purchase conversions for a new product.

| Attribute        | Guidance |
|------------------|----------|
| Banana domain    | Studio (hero shots), Editorial/In Use (lifestyle context) |
| Aspect ratios    | 1:1 (feed), 9:16 (stories/reels), 16:9 (YouTube pre-roll) |
| Copy framework   | AIDA — hook with the problem, reveal the product as the answer |
| CTA              | "Shop Now", "See It In Action", "Get Yours Today" |
| Budget split     | 60% prospecting (broad/LAL), 40% retargeting warm audiences |
| Test priority    | Visual style (photography vs. lifestyle) → headline hook |

**Creative notes:**
- Lead with the product transformation, not specs
- Show the product in context (in-use) within first 3 seconds for video
- Generate hero image first; use as style reference for all variants

---

### 2. Sale / Promotion

**Goal:** Drive immediate conversions with urgency-based messaging.

| Attribute        | Guidance |
|------------------|----------|
| Banana domain    | Studio (clean product + price overlay), Floating (minimal distraction) |
| Aspect ratios    | 1:1 (feed), 4:5 (Meta feed), 9:16 (stories) |
| Copy framework   | PAS — Pain (full price), Agitate (limited time), Solution (discount) |
| CTA              | "Shop the Sale", "Claim Your Discount", "Ends Sunday" |
| Budget split     | 30% prospecting, 70% retargeting (cart abandoners, past purchasers) |
| Test priority    | Discount framing (% off vs. $ off vs. "lowest price") → urgency copy |

**Creative notes:**
- Price/discount must be visible in first frame (no bait-and-switch)
- Use countdown language only if deadline is real
- Avoid cluttered overlays — one value prop per creative

---

### 3. Seasonal

**Goal:** Capture demand spikes (Black Friday, holidays, back-to-school, etc.).

| Attribute        | Guidance |
|------------------|----------|
| Banana domain    | Editorial/Lifestyle (seasonal context), Ingredient (gifting/unboxing) |
| Aspect ratios    | 1:1, 9:16, 16:9 (mix formats for cross-platform reach) |
| Copy framework   | 4P — Picture (seasonal scene), Promise (the gift), Prove (social proof), Push (CTA) |
| CTA              | "Gift It", "Shop Holiday Deals", "Order Before [Date]" |
| Budget split     | Ramp up 2–3 weeks before peak; 50/50 prospecting vs. retargeting at peak |
| Test priority    | Seasonal theme intensity (subtle vs. explicit) → delivery deadline urgency |

**Creative notes:**
- Prepare creatives 4 weeks in advance — CPMs spike at season start
- Seasonal imagery should complement, not overpower, the product
- Include delivery cutoff dates where relevant

---

### 4. Retargeting

**Goal:** Re-engage visitors, cart abandoners, and past purchasers.

| Attribute        | Guidance |
|------------------|----------|
| Banana domain    | Studio (product detail), Floating (minimalist reminder) |
| Aspect ratios    | 1:1 (feed), 9:16 (stories) |
| Copy framework   | BAB — Before (they left without buying), After (what they'd gain), Bridge (return CTA) |
| CTA              | "Still Thinking About It?", "Your Cart Is Waiting", "Complete Your Order" |
| Budget split     | 100% retargeting; segment by recency (1–3 days vs. 7–14 days vs. 30 days) |
| Test priority    | Personalization level (generic vs. product-specific) → incentive (free shipping vs. discount) |

**Creative notes:**
- Show the exact product the user viewed when possible
- Frequency cap: 3–5 impressions/week to avoid fatigue
- Exclude recent purchasers from cart-abandonment segments

---

### 5. Brand Awareness

**Goal:** Build top-of-funnel recognition and affinity with cold audiences.

| Attribute        | Guidance |
|------------------|----------|
| Banana domain    | Editorial/Lifestyle (aspirational), In Use (authentic), Ingredient (brand story) |
| Aspect ratios    | 16:9 (YouTube, connected TV), 9:16 (TikTok, Reels), 1:1 (display) |
| Copy framework   | Star-Story-Solution — introduce brand character, tell origin/values story, connect to product |
| CTA              | "Learn More", "Discover [Brand]", "See Our Story" |
| Budget split     | 80% prospecting (broad targeting), 20% video view retargeting |
| Test priority    | Narrative angle (founder story vs. customer story vs. product origin) |

**Creative notes:**
- Optimize for video view rate and brand recall, not ROAS
- Minimum 15-second video; 30–60 seconds for YouTube
- Avoid hard sell — awareness creatives that lead with price underperform

## Budget Allocation by Campaign Mix

| Business Stage       | Launch | Sale | Seasonal | Retargeting | Awareness |
|----------------------|--------|------|----------|-------------|-----------|
| New brand (<6 mo)    | 40%    | 10%  | 10%      | 10%         | 30%       |
| Growing (6–24 mo)    | 25%    | 20%  | 15%      | 25%         | 15%       |
| Established (>24 mo) | 15%    | 25%  | 20%      | 30%         | 10%       |

## Banana Domain Mode Quick Reference

| Mode            | Best For                          |
|-----------------|-----------------------------------|
| Studio          | Hero shots, sale overlays, PDPs   |
| Floating        | Minimalist retargeting, reminders |
| Ingredient      | Gifting, unboxing, brand story    |
| In Use          | Lifestyle, tutorials, demos       |
| Lifestyle       | Awareness, seasonal, aspiration   |

## Creative Fatigue Signals

- CTR drops >30% week-over-week → rotate creative
- Frequency >5 in 7 days for retargeting → expand audience or pause
- Comment sentiment turns negative → audit messaging and visuals
- CPM increases >25% without audience change → creative refresh needed
